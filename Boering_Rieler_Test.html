Boering-Rieler Test

The Boering-Rieler Test, devised by Charlie Boering and Jane Rieler, aims to deduce whether a living creature exhibiting behaviour indicative of consciousness (i.e. negative reactions to harm) indeed has conscious experience. A creature that fails the test is known as a philosophical zombie (or "p-zombie").
An entity that fails the test is no more than a realistic automata. They sense pain like a camera senses light. They may seem to express emotions, but there is nothing behind those eyes.
Jane Rieler
