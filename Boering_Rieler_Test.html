Boering-Rieler Test

The Boering-Rieler Test, devised by Charlie Boering and Jane Rieler, aims to deduce whether a living creature exhibiting behaviour indicative of consciousness (i.e. negative reactions to harm) indeed has conscious experience. A creature that fails the test is known as a philosophical zombie (or "p-zombie").

Rieler used the Boering-Rieler Test as the basis for her doll machine thought experiment.

Procedure

two halls, near the end of either is an electronic screen displaying the end of the other as if it were being viewed from that hallway. a member of the species being tested is restrained such that they can simultaneously look down both hallways, then another member is killed at the end of one hallway. the restrained member is then let free to run down one hallway of their choice. if they pick the one with the screen depicting the death, they are conscious. if they pick the one that actually has the death but merely displays a blank area, they are consciousless.

Shortcomings

the test requires the subject be capable of locomotion; this effectively excludes plants from testing.

The test also requires two creatures, one of which is required to be killed, thus excluding Class-C entities.
