Doll machine

A doll machine is a hypothetical contraption first described by Jane Rieler in 2006 that takes in inorganic matter and creates a living, albeit consciousless, creature.

Rieler uses the term doll in multiple talks, the earliest in 2002, as an analogy for "something that resembles a conscious being, but isn't one itself." This analogy is what led her to propose the doll machine thought experiment.
