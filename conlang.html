Conlang

Very simple language intended for habitual practice/use (by me).

SOV word order.

Labial Alveolar Palatal Velar Glottal
Nasal m n ng
Plosive p t k
Fricative f v s h
Liquid w l j

The vowels are /a, i, u/. Diphthongs are not phonemic but may occur as realizations of sequential vowels in rapid speech.

Minimal pairs of /v/ and /w/ exist (ex. vahi "woman," wahi "storm"), however the distinction is not always maintained or necessary. However, before /u/ only [v] appears, with [wu] never appearing.

Pronouns
  • ina | 1st person "I"
  • ka | 2nd person "you"
  • ngaka | 3rd person "they"
Nouns
  • ya | food; wellness
  • ma | mouth
  • pi | eye
  • hasa | human
  • vahi | woman
  • wahi | storm
  • malaf | ?
Verbs
  • ? | to be
  • yalang | to eat; to do well, to succeed
  • malang | to speak, to say
  • pilang | to see
Case Particles
  • i | Nominative (follows the subject of a sentence) ex. Hasa i malang. "A person speaks."
  • na | Accusative (follows the object of a sentence) ex. Hasa i ya na yalang. "A person eats food."
Derivational Affixes
  • -lang | noun to verb
Ideophones

Ideophones are rather tricky to get used to as an English speaker; The way they work here is like a "comment" on a phrase. By putting the ideophone next to another word or phrase, it adds meaning in the same way as an adjective or verb (however, the rules for ideophones are much more lenient than these). ex. Ina pin-pin. "That confused me." (lit. I blink-sound).

  • pin-pin | blinking (especially in confusion)
