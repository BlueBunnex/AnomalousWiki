Chinese Government Funds New Gaming Branch of Military

Susanna Bolden

The Chinese government has announced the establishment of a new gaming branch within the People’s Liberation Army (PLA). The branch, which will be known as the National Defense Esports Research Center (NDERC), will be responsible for developing and implementing esports training programs for PLA soldiers, as well as researching the potential use of esports in military operations.

The NDERC is the latest in a series of moves by the Chinese government to leverage the growing popularity of esports for military purposes. In recent years, the PLA has been increasingly investing in esports infrastructure and training programs, and has even established its own esports team.

The Chinese government’s interest in esports is not unfounded. Esports is a rapidly growing industry with a global audience of billions of people. The games themselves are also becoming increasingly sophisticated, and can be used to simulate a wide range of real-world scenarios.

As a result, the Chinese government believes that esports can be used to train soldiers in a variety of skills, such as teamwork, strategic thinking, and decision-making. The government also believes that esports can be used to develop new technologies that can be used in military operations, such as artificial intelligence and virtual reality.

The establishment of the NDERC is a sign of the Chinese government’s commitment to using esports for military purposes. It is also a reminder of the growing importance of esports in China and around the world.
